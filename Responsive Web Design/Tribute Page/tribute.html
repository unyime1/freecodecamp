Ali ibn Abi Talib

Ali ibn Abi Talib (Arabic: عَلِيّ ٱبْن أَبِي طَالِب‎, ʿAlīy ibn ʾAbī Ṭālib; 13 September 601 – 29 January 661) was a cousin and son-in-law of the Islamic prophet Muhammad, who ruled as the fourth caliph from 656 to 661, but is regarded as the rightful immediate successor to Muhammad as an Imam by Shia Muslims

Respected not only as a warrior and leader, but as a writer, religious authority, role model and commander of the faithful.

A Timeline of Ali's Life

  • 601 AD Born in Mecca
  • 610 AD Became the first male to embrace Islam
  • 622 AD Risked his life by thwarting an assasination attempt against Muhammad
  • 624 AD Defeated the Meccan champion Walid ibn Utba in the Battle of Badr
  • 625 AD Played a major role in the Battle of Uhud
  • 627 AD Commanded the muslim army in the Battle of the Trench, where he defeated the legendary Arab warrior Amr ibn Abd al-Wud
  • 628 AD Was Instructed to write down the Treaty of Hudaybiyyah, the peace treaty between Muhammad and the Quraysh
  • 630 AD Protected Muhammad in the Battle of Hunayn
  • 630 AD Played a major role in the Muslim conquest of Mecca
  • 632 AD Played a prominent role in the burial of Muhammad
  • 656 AD Became the fourth Caliph of the muslim empire
  • 661 AD Was assasinated while praying in the Great Mosque of Kufa
